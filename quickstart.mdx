This guide walks you through everything you need to go from a blank slate to a working knowledge base. By the end, you will have created a notebook, written and saved a note, applied tags to it, and found it again using search — the core loop you will repeat every day. The whole process takes about five minutes.

## Set Up Your Knowledge Base

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the App">
    Launch the Personal Knowledge Base in your browser by navigating to **app.yourknowledgebase.com**, or open the desktop app by double-clicking the **Personal KB** icon in your Applications folder (macOS) or Start menu (Windows).

    If this is your first visit, you will be prompted to create a free account or sign in. You can also continue as a guest to try the app without signing up — your notes will be saved locally on that device.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create Your First Notebook">
    In the left sidebar, click **+ New Notebook**. A text field appears inline — type a name for your notebook (for example, `Personal Projects` or `Research`) and press **Enter**.

    Your new notebook is now listed in the sidebar and is automatically selected, ready for your first note.

    <Tip>
      Give your first notebook a broad name. You can always create more focused notebooks later and move notes between them.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Write Your First Note">
    With your notebook selected, click **+ New Note** at the top of the note list (or press **N** on your keyboard). A blank editor opens on the right.

    Type a title in the **Title** field at the top, then click into the body and start writing. The editor supports both **Markdown** and **rich text** — use whichever feels natural.

    ```markdown
    # My First Note

    This is the beginning of my second brain.

    ## Ideas
    - Capture everything
    - Organize with tags and notebooks
    - Link related notes together
    ```

    When you are done, save your note with **Cmd+S** (macOS) or **Ctrl+S** (Windows/Linux). The app also auto-saves every few seconds, so you will never lose your work.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Tags to the Note">
    Below the note title, click the **Tags** field (it shows a small tag icon and placeholder text that reads *Add a tag…*).

    Type a tag name — for example, `ideas` — and press **Enter**. The tag is created and attached to the note. Add as many tags as you like by typing another name and pressing **Enter** again.

    Tags appear as colored chips in the Tags field. You can click any tag chip to see all other notes that share that tag.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Search for the Note">
    Press **Cmd+K** (macOS) or **Ctrl+K** (Windows/Linux) from anywhere in the app to open the **Search** palette. You can also click the **magnifying glass** icon in the top toolbar.

    Type any keyword from your note's title or body. Results appear instantly as you type, with matching text highlighted. Click a result to jump directly to that note.

    Search works across every note, notebook, tag, and heading in your knowledge base — you do not need to remember where you filed something.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Use templates to jump-start your notes and skip the blank-page feeling. See the [Templates guide](/guides/templates) to learn how to apply built-in templates or create your own.
</Tip>
