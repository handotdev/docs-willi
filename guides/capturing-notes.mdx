Fast capture is the foundation of a useful knowledge base. The longer you wait to write something down, the more detail you lose. This guide walks you through every method available for getting ideas into your notes — from a full editor session to a one-line Quick Capture — so you can record thoughts at the speed they arrive.

## Ways to Create a Note

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click the New Note Button">
    In the left sidebar, click the **+ New Note** button at the top of your notebook list. A blank note opens in the editor, ready for a title and body.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use the Keyboard Shortcut">
    Press **Cmd+N** (macOS) or **Ctrl+N** (Windows/Linux) from anywhere in the app to instantly open a new note in your current notebook. This is the fastest way to start writing without leaving the keyboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the Quick Capture Panel">
    Press **Cmd+Shift+N** (macOS) or **Ctrl+Shift+N** (Windows/Linux) to open the Quick Capture panel. This floating input works even when the app is in the background, so you can capture a thought without switching windows.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Writing Tips

A few habits will make your notes more useful over time:

- **Title first.** Write a clear, specific title before anything else. A good title makes search and linking much easier later.
- **Body second.** Add detail, context, or supporting material in the note body. Even a single sentence is enough to preserve the idea.
- **Use checklists for action items.** Start a line with `- [ ]` to create a checkbox. Check items off directly in the note as you complete them.
- **Use code blocks for code.** Wrap code in triple backticks with a language tag so it renders with syntax highlighting and stays readable.

## Auto-Save Behavior

You never need to manually save a note. The app saves your work automatically every few seconds as you type. Watch the toolbar — a small **Saved** indicator appears after each auto-save cycle. If you see **Saving…**, give it a moment before closing the app or navigating away.

<Tip>
  Keep a notebook called **Inbox** for unprocessed captures — review and organize it weekly. This lets you capture freely without stopping to decide where something belongs.
</Tip>

## Quick Capture Feature

The Quick Capture panel is designed for one-line ideas that need a home but don't need immediate attention. When you open it with **Cmd+Shift+N** / **Ctrl+Shift+N**, type your idea and press **Enter**. The note lands automatically in your **Inbox** notebook with a timestamp, ready to be expanded or organized during your next review session.

Quick Capture keeps your focus on what you're doing while ensuring no idea goes unrecorded.
