A well-organized knowledge base is one you actually use. When you can find what you need in seconds, you trust the system and capture more freely. This guide shows you how to structure your notebooks, choose tags that scale, and run a simple weekly review that keeps everything manageable without becoming a chore.

## Recommended Workflow: Inbox → Notebooks → Tags

Everything starts in the **Inbox** notebook — captured quickly, organized deliberately. The weekly review is the habit that keeps that flow working.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Your Inbox">
    Navigate to the **Inbox** notebook in the sidebar. This is where all Quick Captures and unprocessed notes land.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read Each Note">
    Open each note one at a time. Read it fully and decide whether it's worth keeping and, if so, what it belongs to.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Move It to a Notebook">
    Drag the note into the appropriate notebook, or right-click it and choose **Move to Notebook**. If no notebook fits, consider whether a new one is warranted — but err toward using an existing one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Tags">
    Add one or two tags that describe the note's status, topic, or relevant people. Keep the tag count small — more tags rarely means better retrieval.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Archive or Delete Unneeded Notes">
    If a note is no longer relevant, move it to your **Archive** notebook instead of deleting it. If it's truly worthless, delete it. A smaller, higher-quality knowledge base beats a large cluttered one.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Notebook Structure

Use broad buckets rather than deeply nested folders. A flat structure is easier to scan and reduces the friction of deciding where something belongs.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Work" icon="briefcase">
    Projects, meeting notes, decisions, client research, and anything related to your professional life.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Personal" icon="user">
    Goals, journaling, household projects, finances, health notes, and anything outside work.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Learning" icon="book-open">
    Book notes, course summaries, articles worth revisiting, and concepts you're actively studying.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Archive" icon="box-archive">
    Completed projects, outdated reference material, and anything you want to keep but no longer actively reference.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Tag Strategy

Tags work best when they follow a consistent convention. Three categories cover most use cases:

- **Status tags** — `#todo`, `#in-progress`, `#done`, `#waiting`. Use these to track the state of action-oriented notes.
- **Topic tags** — `#python`, `#design`, `#marketing`, `#ux`. Use these for subject matter that cuts across multiple notebooks.
- **Person tags** — `#alice`, `#bob`, `#team-design`. Use these to surface all notes related to a specific person or group.

Stick to lowercase and hyphens for multi-word tags so your tag list stays predictable and easy to type.

<Note>
  Avoid over-organizing — a note in the right notebook with one or two good tags is enough. Spending more time organizing than writing defeats the purpose of a knowledge base.
</Note>

## Archiving Notes

When a project wraps up or a reference becomes outdated, move those notes to your **Archive** notebook rather than deleting them. Archived notes remain fully searchable and linkable — you're just moving them out of your active working set. If you later realize you need something, it's still there.

To archive a note quickly, right-click it in the sidebar and choose **Move to Notebook → Archive**.
