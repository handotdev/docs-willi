Notebooks are the top-level organizational containers in your Personal Knowledge Base. Think of each notebook as a dedicated folder for a specific topic, project, or area of your life — a place where all the notes that belong together can live side by side. While tags let you label notes across multiple themes, a notebook gives you a clear home base for a coherent body of work.

## Creating a Notebook

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the sidebar">
    If the sidebar is collapsed, click the **Sidebar** icon in the top-left corner or press <kbd>Cmd</kbd>+<kbd>\\</kbd> (macOS) / <kbd>Ctrl</kbd>+<kbd>\\</kbd> (Windows/Linux) to expand it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click New Notebook">
    At the bottom of the notebook list, click **+ New Notebook**. A text field appears inline in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter a name and confirm">
    Type a descriptive name for your notebook, then press <kbd>Enter</kbd> to create it. The notebook appears immediately in alphabetical order in the sidebar.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Consider creating one notebook per project or life area — for example, **Work**, **Personal**, **Learning**, or **Side Projects**. Keeping notebooks broad and using tags for finer detail prevents you from ending up with dozens of notebooks that are hard to navigate.
</Tip>

## Moving Notes Between Notebooks

You can reassign a note to a different notebook at any time using either of two methods.

**Drag and drop** — In the note list, grab a note and drag it onto the target notebook name in the sidebar. A highlight appears around the notebook to confirm the drop target before you release.

**Note settings menu** — Open the note you want to move, then click the **⋯ More Options** button in the note toolbar. Select **Move to Notebook** and choose the destination from the dropdown list.

## Nesting Notebooks

You can create sub-notebooks to add a second level of hierarchy beneath any existing notebook. This is useful when a broad area — like **Work** — contains several distinct projects that each deserve their own space.

To create a sub-notebook, right-click an existing notebook in the sidebar and select **New Sub-Notebook**. Sub-notebooks appear indented under their parent and can be collapsed or expanded by clicking the arrow beside the parent notebook name.

<Note>
  Every note belongs to exactly one notebook, but can have multiple tags. Nesting is limited to one level — you cannot create a sub-sub-notebook.
</Note>

## Deleting a Notebook

<Steps>
  <Step title="Right-click the notebook">
    In the sidebar, right-click (or long-press on touch devices) the notebook you want to remove.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Delete Notebook">
    Choose **Delete Notebook** from the context menu. A confirmation dialog appears.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the deletion">
    Click **Delete** to confirm. The notebook is removed from the sidebar.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Deleting a notebook does **not** delete the notes inside it. All notes that were assigned to the deleted notebook are automatically moved to the **Unfiled** notebook, where you can review and reassign them at your own pace.
</Warning>

## The Unfiled Notebook

**Unfiled** is a built-in notebook that acts as a catch-all. Notes land here when they are created before you choose a notebook, or when their assigned notebook is deleted. You cannot rename or delete the Unfiled notebook, but you can move notes out of it at any time.
